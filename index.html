About

Hi there, I am Gail!

I am an Executive Director & VP of City Year, Columbia. City Year partners with public schools in high-need communities across the U.S. and international affiliates. AmeriCorps members provide research-based student, classroom and school wide support to help students stay in school and on track to graduate. In addition to my nonprofit work, I am an owner of Anchor Shred & Recycle Co. I hold a BA in Education, an MS in Leadership and Policy and a PhD in Applied Management and Decision Sciences. I am a 2004 Schott Foundation Fellow. My original one-woman show, “It’s Cloudy in the West” based on family history circa 1890, toured extensively. I have published two books for The History Press, Drink Small; The Life & Music of South Carolina’s Blues Doctor and Carolina Bluegrass; A High Lonesome History. Gail’s latest project, The Tao of Reduce, Reuse, Recycle is a collection of “existentialist whimsy”.
